FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is this?

We're hosting a Hackathon in the University of Sheffield, but it's open to anyone in the country! We'll provide WiFi, power and food while you and your team create something new.

What's a 'Hackathon'?

A Hackathon is a programming marathon, where teams of 2-4 put their ideas together to create something exciting in only 24 hours. It gives teams of hackers a chance to turn their ideas into reality.

Can I attend?

You can attend if you're a student or you have left university after the 19th March 2015. Under 18? Once we can confirm that we have safeguard measures in place, we will be able to welcome under 18s. We'll update you on this closer to the time.

How do I register?

Registration isn't open yet, but you can register your interest and we will notify you when more information is available.

Will I be photographed?

There will be a photographer at the event, however please let us know if you do not wish to be photographed.

Code of Conduct

We will be following the MLH Code of Conduct. Should you have any problems, don't hesitate to let a member of staff know.

What about sleep?

If you need to sleep, we will be providing quiet rooms where a few people can get some Z's. Ahead of the event, ensure that you have a good nights sleep so that you are well rested for the 24 hours ahead (plus the awards!).

But I don't have a team?

If you can, get a team together before the event but if that's not possible, you can find people to team up with before the Hackathon starts during the introduction.

What do I need to bring?

Your ticket. A laptop is a must. Don't forget the charger. That's about it.
We can only accept Laptops, Tablets and Mobiles connected to power sockets.

If we haven't managed to answer your question, feel free to ask us at team@hacksheffield.co.
